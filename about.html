About Matthew

You can take a look at my résumé here:

My Résumé



I've done UX Design for about four years now (in design, research, and testing roles) on my own and as a part of larger teams.

What I bring to the table, as a UX Designer or Information Architect:

  • I'm good with UX tools, like AxureRP, Sketch, Balsamiq, etc.; but understand too that UX is more about process and user knowledge than tool knowledge.
  • I have UX education from George Brown College.
  • I'm good with graphic design tools, like Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, etc.
  • I can code; I can write HTML, CSS (and Sass), JavaScript (and jQuery, Node, etc.), PHP, SQL, etc.
  • I'm well-versed in accessibility legislation (Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA) and WCAG 2.0)
  • I have certification for leading group sessions from the YWCA.
  • I have empathy for people.

What I'm looking for:

  • A full-time position as a UX Designer or Information Architect (in-house, agency) where I can grow alongside a business.
  • UX consulting work (I'll do heuristic evaluations of your product for a modest fee (or, if you represent a not-for-profit, for free).
  • To connect with other interesting professionals in the field via social networking or email.

Anyway: I hope that this answers some of the questions you have about me. I'm a hard worker and I'm good at what I do. If you'd like to know more, or even to chat about UX, just email me, or connect with me on social media (various links are below).

I hope to hear from you, you awesome whoever.
